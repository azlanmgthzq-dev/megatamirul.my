# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Recruiters, hiring managers, and engineering leads evaluating Megat Amirul as a full-stack software engineer candidate. They land on the site with limited time (skimming, not reading deeply), are comparing against other candidates, and are looking for evidence of real production experience — not just claimed skills.

## Product Purpose

A personal portfolio site that establishes Megat as a capable full-stack software engineer with proven experience shipping production interfaces for complex, regulated systems (aerospace & defence). It exists to convert a recruiter's first look into a callback: showcase real projects, real problem-solving (the Engineering Sharing page), education, and certifications, backed by a working contact path.

## Brand Personality

Precise, technical, understated. Confidence through evidence (real bugs solved, real trade-offs made) rather than hype or decoration. Dark, focused, engineering-grade — the interface should feel like it was built by someone who understands both systems and craft, not like a templated resume site.

## Anti-references

No specific external anti-references — calibrate against the site's own current baseline: dark background, `glass`/`noise` utility surfaces, cyan-primary/teal-accent palette, Space Grotesk display type. Avoid generic SaaS/AI-slop patterns: purple gradients, gradient text, side-stripe card borders, tiny uppercase tracked eyebrows above every section, identical icon-card grids.

## Design Principles

- Evidence over adjectives — every claim of skill should be backed by a concrete, specific example (a real bug, a real trade-off), not a buzzword.
- Regulated-systems depth is a supporting detail, not the headline — the site reads as "full-stack engineer" first, with aerospace/defence experience surfacing as proof of rigor.
- Quiet confidence — restrained color and motion, no decorative flourishes that don't carry meaning.
- Consistency across surfaces — new pages (e.g. Engineering Sharing) must feel native to the existing tab/glass/noise visual language, not bolted on.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No formal WCAG target specified; default to WCAG AA practice (body text contrast ≥4.5:1, visible focus states, reduced-motion alternatives for scroll/entrance animations).
